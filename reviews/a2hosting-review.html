Introduction & Company Background

A2 Hosting has built its reputation on one primary promise: speed. Founded in 2001 by Bryan Muthig in Ann Arbor, Michigan (hence the "A2" name), the company has grown from a small hosting provider to a speed-focused powerhouse serving customers worldwide.

What sets A2 Hosting apart is their Turbo Server technology, which they claim delivers up to 20x faster page loads compared to standard hosting. This focus on performance has made them particularly popular among developers and speed-conscious website owners.

A2 Hosting remains independently owned, which is increasingly rare in an industry dominated by large conglomerates. This independence has allowed them to maintain their focus on performance and developer-friendly features without corporate pressure to cut costs.

In this review, we'll test A2 Hosting's speed claims and evaluate whether their Turbo servers deliver on their promises. We'll also examine their unique anytime money-back guarantee and developer features.

Features Deep Dive

Turbo Server Technology

A2's Turbo servers use LiteSpeed web server technology with built-in caching, NVMe SSD storage, and optimized configurations. The result is significantly faster page loads compared to traditional Apache-based hosting.

Developer Features

A2 Hosting is developer-friendly with SSH access, Git integration, WP-CLI support, and multiple PHP versions. They support Python, Ruby, Node.js, and Perl. Staging environments are available for testing changes before going live.

Security Features

All plans include free SSL certificates, perpetual security updates, and HackScan protection. Their Turbo plans include additional security features like advanced DDoS protection and enhanced firewall rules.

Global Data Centers

A2 Hosting offers data centers in Michigan (USA), Arizona (USA), Amsterdam (Europe), and Singapore (Asia-Pacific). This allows you to choose a server location closest to your target audience for optimal performance.
