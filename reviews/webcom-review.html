Introduction & Company Background

Web.com has been helping small businesses establish their online presence since 1999. Originally founded as Website Pros, the company has evolved through various acquisitions and rebrandings to become a comprehensive web services provider focused primarily on small business customers.

What distinguishes Web.com from traditional hosting providers is their emphasis on done-for-you services. While you can certainly build your own website, Web.com also offers professional design services where their team creates your site for you. This hands-off approach appeals to busy business owners who want a professional web presence without the DIY learning curve.

In 2018, Web.com was acquired by Siris Capital, and in 2020, they merged with Newfold Digital (the parent company of Bluehost and HostGator). Despite these corporate changes, Web.com has maintained its focus on serving small businesses with simplified web solutions.

In this review, we'll examine Web.com's hosting and website builder services based on hands-on testing. We'll help you determine if their beginner-friendly approach is right for your small business needs.

Features Deep Dive

Website Builder

Web.com's website builder is designed for simplicity. The drag-and-drop interface requires no coding knowledge and includes industry-specific templates. You can create a basic business website in under an hour.

Professional Design Services

For those who prefer a hands-off approach, Web.com offers professional website design services. Their team will create a custom website based on your requirements, handling everything from design to content placement.

Marketing Tools

Web.com includes various marketing tools with their plans, including basic SEO tools, email marketing capabilities, and social media integration. These help small businesses promote their online presence.

E-commerce Features

For businesses selling online, Web.com offers e-commerce functionality including product listings, shopping cart, and payment processing through integration with major payment providers.
