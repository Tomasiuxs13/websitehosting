Introduction & Company Background

Bluehost holds a unique position in the web hosting industry as one of only three hosting providers officially recommended by WordPress.org. This endorsement, which has been in place since 2005, carries significant weight in the WordPress community and has helped Bluehost become one of the most popular hosting choices for WordPress websites worldwide.

Founded in 2003 by Matt Heaton in Provo, Utah, Bluehost has grown to power over 2 million websites globally. In 2010, the company was acquired by Endurance International Group (now Newfold Digital), which also owns HostGator, making them sister companies. Despite the corporate ownership, Bluehost has maintained its distinct identity and continues to focus heavily on WordPress optimization.

The WordPress.org recommendation isn't given lightly. To earn this endorsement, Bluehost had to demonstrate exceptional WordPress compatibility, reliable performance, and a commitment to the WordPress community. They've maintained this relationship for nearly two decades by continuously improving their WordPress-specific features and support.

In this comprehensive review, we'll examine whether Bluehost lives up to its reputation in 2025. We signed up for their Choice Plus plan, the most popular option, and tested performance, support, and features over several months. Whether you're launching your first WordPress blog or migrating an established site, this review will help you make an informed decision.

The hosting landscape has evolved significantly since Bluehost's founding, with new competitors emerging and existing players improving their offerings. Let's see how Bluehost stacks up against the competition in today's market.

Features Deep Dive

WordPress Integration

Bluehost's WordPress integration is where they truly shine. When you sign up, WordPress is automatically installed and ready to use – no manual installation required. The custom WordPress dashboard within Bluehost provides easy access to themes, plugins, and site management tools without needing to navigate through cPanel for common tasks.

The integration includes a curated marketplace of WordPress themes and plugins, automatic WordPress core updates (with the option to disable), and WordPress-specific caching for improved performance. Staging environments are available on higher-tier plans, allowing you to test changes before pushing them to your live site.

Hosting Types Available

Bluehost offers a comprehensive range of hosting solutions. Their shared hosting is the entry point for most users, providing affordable hosting suitable for small to medium websites. WordPress hosting is essentially optimized shared hosting with WordPress-specific enhancements. For growing sites, WooCommerce hosting provides e-commerce optimizations, while VPS and dedicated hosting offer more resources and control for high-traffic sites.

Control Panel Experience

Bluehost uses a customized version of cPanel that's been streamlined for ease of use. The interface is more beginner-friendly than standard cPanel, with common tasks prominently displayed. You can manage domains, email accounts, databases, and files through an intuitive interface. The Bluehost-specific dashboard provides quick access to WordPress management, marketing tools, and account settings.

Security Features

Security is a priority at Bluehost. All plans include free SSL certificates from Let's Encrypt, with automatic renewal. Their servers are protected by custom security rules, and they offer SiteLock security scanning as an add-on for malware detection and removal. CodeGuard automated backups are available for an additional fee, providing daily backups with one-click restore functionality.

For WordPress users, Bluehost provides automatic security updates and includes basic spam protection. The Choice Plus plan and above include domain privacy protection, keeping your personal information out of public WHOIS records.

Website Builder

Bluehost includes their proprietary website builder on all plans. This drag-and-drop builder is designed for users who want to create a website without coding knowledge. While WordPress itself is more powerful and flexible, the Bluehost builder can be useful for simple sites or landing pages. It includes a selection of templates and basic customization options.

Email Hosting

Bluehost provides professional email hosting with your domain. You can create multiple email addresses (the number varies by plan) and access them through webmail, POP3, or IMAP. Spam filtering is included to keep your inbox clean. For businesses needing more advanced email features, Microsoft 365 integration is available at an additional cost.

Customer Support Analysis

Bluehost offers comprehensive 24/7 support through multiple channels: phone, live chat, and email. This multi-channel approach ensures you can get help however you prefer to communicate.

Phone Support

Bluehost provides toll-free phone support for US customers. During our testing, we experienced hold times ranging from 5-20 minutes depending on the time of day. Weekend and late-night calls typically had shorter wait times. The agents were generally knowledgeable about WordPress-specific issues, which is a significant advantage for WordPress users.

Live Chat

Live chat is accessible directly from the Bluehost dashboard and website. Wait times were typically under 10 minutes during our tests. Chat support is particularly useful for quick questions or issues that can be resolved with text-based communication. The agents can share links to relevant help articles and walk you through solutions step by step.

Knowledge Base

Bluehost maintains an extensive knowledge base with hundreds of articles covering everything from basic setup to advanced configurations. The WordPress-specific documentation is particularly comprehensive, reflecting their focus on WordPress hosting. Video tutorials are available for visual learners, and the search functionality makes it easy to find relevant articles.

Support Rating: 8.5/10

Bluehost's support is solid, particularly for WordPress-related issues. The availability of phone support is a significant advantage over some competitors. Wait times can be lengthy during peak hours, but the quality of assistance is generally good.
